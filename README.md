# Bank Churn Analysis Dashboard

This project explores customer churn patterns using an interactive Power BI dashboard.
The goal of this analysis is to understand how churn varies across different countries and identify patterns that might explain why customers leave.

The dashboard focuses on three countries: France, Spain, and Germany.
It analyzes customer attributes such as age, credit score, balance, tenure, product count, and activity status to identify potential churn trends.

## Tools Used

* Power BI
* Excel

## Key Insights

**France**

* Churn rate is about **16%**.
* Most churned customers are in the **35–60 age group**.
* Many churned customers have **only one product**.
* Around **70% of churned customers also have credit cards**.

**Spain**

* Churn rate is about **17%**, slightly higher than France.
* A similar pattern appears where **single-product customers churn more frequently**.

**Germany**

* Churn rate is **32%**, which is significantly higher than the other two countries.
* Many churned customers also have **higher account balances**, which may indicate potential loss of valuable customers.

## Simple Observations

* Customers with **only one product** appear more likely to churn.
* The **35–60 age group** appears frequently among churned customers.
* **Germany shows the highest churn rate**, which may require further investigation.

## Possible Recommendations

* Encourage customers to **use multiple banking products** to increase engagement.
* Focus retention strategies on **high-balance customers**, especially in Germany.
* Improve engagement with customers who show **lower activity levels**.
